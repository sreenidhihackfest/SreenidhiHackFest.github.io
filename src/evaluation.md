## Evaluation Scheme

The evaluation process is designed to ensure fairness and transparency. Each project will be judged based on the following key areas during the single round of the hackathon:

1. **Clarity & Scope**  
   - Is the problem clearly understood and addressed?

2. **Innovation**  
   - Is the solution original and creative?

3. **Feasibility**  
   - Is the idea feasible and relevant?

4. **Engineering Skills**  
   - Are technical skills effectively demonstrated?

5. **Adherence to Problem Statement**  
   - Is the solution aligned with the problem statement?

6. **Implementation & User Experience**  
   - Is the solution well-implemented and user-friendly?

7. **Completion**  
   - Is the solution complete?

8. **Teamwork**  
   - Did the team collaborate effectively?

9. **Q&A**  
   - Did the team explain and answer questions well?

10. **Overall Impression**  
   - What is the overall impression of the project?

---

## Judging Flow

1. **Project Submission**
   - Submit projects with a description, code link, and any supporting materials by the deadline.

2. **Single Round Presentation**
   - Teams will present their projects over the course of the 2-day hackathon.
   - Presentations will focus on the project's implementation, impact, and overall quality.
   - Judges will provide feedback and evaluate projects based on the criteria.

3. **Scoring**
   - Judges will score each project based on the evaluation criteria outlined above.
   - Scores will determine the final rankings.

4. **Announcement of Winners**
   - Winners will be announced at the closing ceremony on the second day of the hackathon.

---

## Judging Criteria

### 1. Innovation
- Is the solution original and innovative?

### 2. Technical Implementation
- Is the implementation functional and technically sound?

### 3. Impact
- Does the project solve a significant problem and have impact?

### 4. Presentation
- Is the solution clear and well-presented?

### 5. Team Collaboration
- Did the team collaborate and communicate effectively?
